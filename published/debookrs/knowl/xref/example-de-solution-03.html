Example 23.

Determine if \(y= 2e^{-3t}\) is a solution to the DE \(y'' - y' - 12y = 0\text{.}\)
Solution.
As in the previous example, we need to substitute \(y, y',\) and \(y''\) into the DE.
\begin{align*} y \amp= 2e^{-3t} \hspace{-0.4cm}\amp\Rightarrow \hspace{0.1cm} y' \amp= -6e^{-3t} \hspace{-0.4cm}\amp\Rightarrow \hspace{0.1cm} y'' \amp= 18e^{-3t} \end{align*}
Now letâ€™s substitute these expressions into the DE.
\begin{align*} LHS \amp= y'' - y' - 12y \\ \amp= \left(18e^{-3t}\right) - \left( -6e^{-3t} \right) - 12\left( 2e^{-3t} \right) \\ \amp= 18e^{-3t} + 6e^{-3t} - 24e^{-3t} \\ \amp= 0 \\ \amp= RHS \end{align*}
So, yes, \(y= 2e^{-3t}\) is a solution to \(y'' - y' - 12y = 0.\)
